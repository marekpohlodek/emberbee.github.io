02 / PREMISE

The pitch, in one breath.

~ 90s read

A world where the songs of creatures tune the weather, and a quiet wanderer collects them not to win, but to remember.

You play Ember, an apprentice tuner who walks between six biomes following a faint, broken pulse. The creatures aren't trophies — they're co-travelers. Each one remembers what you do (and what you don't), and your team's mood bends combat tempo as much as their stats do.

Combat is real-time ATB. Decisions stack while gauges fill. Hesitate and the music plays without you; rush and you'll miss the beat that opens an enemy's guard. Every fight is a small tempo puzzle.

PILLAR 01

Generous rules.

No bad picks. Every creature has a kit that rewards the player who commits.

PILLAR 02

Music of combat.

Every fight has a tempo. Read the beat, find the opening.

PILLAR 03

They remember.

Creatures track how you treat them — and it bleeds into the dialogue, the stats, the silence.

PILLAR 04

A short, full meal.

~15 hour campaign. Roguelite encore for after. No filler.

03 / COMBAT

The ATB clock.

Real-time · pausable

Each combatant has a gauge. When it fills, they act. You don't lose because you picked wrong — you lose because the song moved on without you.

Hold the shoulder to peek the queue, tap to chain. Slow time at a cost. Items, party swaps, and creature songs all live on the same clock as your enemies — so does mercy.

  • A Gauges fill in real time — pause to think, but the clock keeps moving slower.
  • B Read enemy tells in the queue; interrupt with a timed strike.
  • C Songs & emotes spend a beat each — choose what to fill, what to skip.
